REMOTE WORK AND HYBRID WORKPLACE POLICY

Version: 2.5
Effective Date: January 1, 2026
Last Reviewed: January 1, 2026
Policy Owner: Chief Human Resources Officer

SECTION 1: POLICY OVERVIEW

1.1 Introduction and Purpose
Enterprise Solutions Corporation ("Company") recognizes that flexible work arrangements can enhance employee satisfaction, productivity, and work-life balance while supporting business objectives. This Remote Work and Hybrid Workplace Policy ("Policy") establishes guidelines, expectations, and procedures for employees working remotely, whether on a full-time, hybrid, or occasional basis.

This Policy is designed to:
- Define eligible remote work arrangements and approval processes
- Establish performance expectations and accountability measures
- Ensure security, confidentiality, and compliance with company policies
- Support effective communication and collaboration in a distributed workforce
- Maintain organizational culture and employee engagement
- Protect the health, safety, and well-being of remote workers

1.2 Scope and Applicability
This Policy applies to all employees engaged in remote work arrangements, including:
- Full-time remote employees who work from home or other approved locations exclusively
- Hybrid employees who split time between office and remote locations
- Employees who work remotely on an occasional or ad-hoc basis
- Employees traveling for business who work remotely during travel

This Policy does not apply to employees on approved leave or contractors/consultants working under separate agreements.

1.3 Definitions
- "Remote Work" means performing job duties from a location other than a Company office
- "Hybrid Work" means a schedule combining in-office and remote work days
- "Primary Workspace" refers to the employee's designated remote work location
- "Core Hours" are designated times when employees must be available for collaboration
- "Synchronous Work" requires real-time collaboration and communication
- "Asynchronous Work" can be completed independently without immediate interaction

SECTION 2: REMOTE WORK ARRANGEMENTS

2.1 Types of Remote Work Arrangements

FULL-TIME REMOTE
- Employee works remotely 100% of the time
- May be required to come to office for specific meetings, events, or business needs
- Typically requires approval for positions that can be performed entirely remotely
- Subject to geographic restrictions based on business, tax, and legal considerations

HYBRID SCHEDULE
- Employee works some days in office and some days remotely
- Common hybrid schedules include:
  - 3 days in office / 2 days remote per week
  - 2 days in office / 3 days remote per week
  - Flexible hybrid (employee chooses days with manager approval)
  - Fixed hybrid (specific days designated for in-office work)
- Teams are encouraged to have overlapping in-office days to facilitate collaboration

OCCASIONAL REMOTE WORK
- Employee primarily works in office but may work remotely on an occasional basis
- Requires manager approval for each instance
- Appropriate for appointments, home repairs, weather conditions, or personal circumstances

TEMPORARY REMOTE WORK
- Short-term remote work due to special circumstances (e.g., illness recovery, family care, office closure)
- Typically approved for 1-4 weeks
- May transition to formal arrangement if extended

2.2 Eligibility Criteria
Remote work eligibility is determined based on:

POSITION SUITABILITY
- Job duties can be performed effectively without physical presence
- Role does not require frequent in-person interaction, equipment access, or physical tasks
- Performance can be measured by outputs and results rather than physical presence
- Technology and systems support remote execution of responsibilities

EMPLOYEE QUALIFICATIONS
- Employee has completed probationary period (typically 90 days)
- Demonstrates strong performance, meeting or exceeding expectations
- Exhibits self-discipline, time management, and independent work capability
- Communicates effectively and proactively
- Has reliable attendance record and professional conduct
- Possesses necessary technology skills and troubleshooting ability

WORKSPACE REQUIREMENTS
- Dedicated, quiet workspace conducive to productivity
- Reliable high-speed internet connection (minimum 25 Mbps download, 5 Mbps upload)
- Appropriate lighting, ergonomic setup, and minimal distractions
- Professional background for video calls
- Secure environment for confidential work and materials

2.3 Geographic Considerations
- Employees must reside within their approved work state/country
- Remote work from other states requires HR approval due to tax and legal implications
- International remote work requires executive approval and legal review
- Employees must notify HR of any change in primary residence within 10 days
- Company reserves the right to restrict remote work based on business operations, legal compliance, or tax considerations in certain locations

SECTION 3: REMOTE WORK REQUEST AND APPROVAL PROCESS

3.1 Request Procedure
Employees seeking a remote work arrangement should:
1. Assess position and personal suitability using the Remote Work Self-Assessment (available from HR)
2. Discuss the proposed arrangement with their direct manager
3. Complete the Remote Work Request Form with details of the proposed schedule
4. Submit form to manager for review and approval
5. Forward approved request to Human Resources for final review
6. Complete required remote work training upon approval
7. Sign the Remote Work Agreement acknowledging responsibilities and expectations

3.2 Manager Evaluation Factors
Managers will evaluate requests considering:
- Employee's performance history and work style
- Nature of job responsibilities and tasks
- Team collaboration needs and impact on colleagues
- Department workflow and coverage requirements
- Client/customer interaction requirements
- Frequency of in-person meetings or events
- Equipment, tools, and resource needs
- Business operations and organizational needs

3.3 Approval Authority
- Managers approve requests for their direct reports for hybrid and occasional remote work
- Directors or above must approve full-time remote arrangements
- Human Resources reviews all approvals for compliance and consistency
- C-level approval required for out-of-state or international remote work

3.4 Trial Periods
- New remote work arrangements typically include a 90-day trial period
- Performance, productivity, and communication will be evaluated during trial
- Arrangements may be modified or terminated if expectations are not met
- Successful completion of trial period leads to ongoing remote work status

3.5 Modification or Termination of Arrangement
The Company reserves the right to modify or terminate remote work arrangements:
- At any time with reasonable notice for business needs
- Immediately for policy violations, performance issues, or security concerns
- With 30 days notice for changes in position requirements
- Due to changes in business operations, client needs, or organizational structure

SECTION 4: REMOTE WORK EXPECTATIONS AND RESPONSIBILITIES

4.1 Work Hours and Availability
- Remote employees must maintain regular, consistent work hours
- Employees must be available during designated core hours (typically 10 AM - 3 PM local time)
- Work schedule must be communicated to manager and team
- Calendar should reflect availability, meetings, and time blocks
- Response time expectations: Email within 4 hours, instant messages within 30 minutes during work hours
- Time zone differences must be accommodated for meetings and collaboration

4.2 Attendance and Time Tracking
- Remote employees must adhere to the same attendance policies as in-office employees
- Non-exempt employees must accurately record all hours worked using the time tracking system
- Breaks and meal periods must be taken and recorded as required
- Unscheduled absences must be reported to manager immediately
- PTO requests must be submitted and approved in advance through standard procedures

4.3 Performance Standards
Remote employees are held to the same performance standards as in-office employees:
- Meet all deadlines, quality standards, and productivity metrics
- Complete assigned tasks and projects as scheduled
- Participate actively in team meetings and activities
- Respond promptly to communications from managers, colleagues, and clients
- Maintain professional conduct and work ethic
- Achieve goals and objectives as defined in performance plans

Performance will be evaluated based on:
- Quality and timeliness of work output
- Achievement of measurable goals and KPIs
- Communication effectiveness and responsiveness
- Collaboration and teamwork
- Adherence to policies and procedures

4.4 Communication Requirements
Effective communication is critical for remote work success:

VIDEO PRESENCE
- Video must be enabled for all scheduled meetings unless bandwidth limitations exist
- Professional appearance and background required for video calls
- Test video and audio before important meetings

EMAIL AND MESSAGING
- Check email at least three times daily during work hours
- Respond to urgent messages within 30 minutes
- Use instant messaging for quick questions and real-time collaboration
- Set status indicators accurately (available, busy, in meeting, away)

MEETINGS AND COLLABORATION
- Attend all required meetings punctually
- Be present and engaged; minimize multitasking during meetings
- Use mute when not speaking to reduce background noise
- Contribute to discussions and participate actively
- Schedule regular one-on-one meetings with manager (weekly or bi-weekly)
- Participate in team activities, events, and social interactions

DOCUMENTATION
- Document work progress, decisions, and action items thoroughly
- Keep shared documents and project management tools updated
- Provide regular status updates to manager and team
- Share knowledge and information proactively

4.5 In-Office Requirements
Even full-time remote employees may be required to come to the office for:
- All-hands meetings, town halls, and important company events
- Team building activities and department gatherings
- Critical project meetings or collaborative work sessions
- Client meetings or presentations
- Training programs and professional development
- Performance reviews and other HR processes
- Onboarding of new team members

Expectations for in-office attendance:
- Hybrid employees must maintain agreed-upon in-office schedule
- Full-time remote employees may be required to come to office quarterly or as needed
- Reasonable advance notice (typically 2 weeks) will be provided for required office attendance
- Company will not reimburse travel expenses for employees who chose to relocate away from office location
- Local remote employees are expected to come to office when requested with minimal notice

SECTION 5: WORKSPACE AND EQUIPMENT

5.1 Home Office Requirements
Remote employees must establish an appropriate workspace that:
- Is dedicated to work activities with minimal distractions
- Has adequate space for computer, monitors, and work materials
- Provides good lighting to prevent eye strain
- Is ergonomically suitable with appropriate desk, chair, and equipment positioning
- Maintains comfortable temperature and air quality
- Is secure and private for confidential conversations and information
- Complies with local building codes and homeowner/rental agreements

5.2 Company-Provided Equipment
The Company will provide the following equipment for approved remote workers:
- Laptop computer with required software and security tools
- Monitor (typically one; two for roles requiring extensive screen work)
- Keyboard and mouse
- Headset with microphone for calls and meetings
- Docking station if needed
- Mobile phone for roles requiring regular external communication

Equipment remains Company property and must be:
- Used only for business purposes
- Maintained in good working condition
- Returned upon termination or change in remote work status
- Covered under Company insurance (not employee's homeowner policy)

5.3 Employee-Provided Resources
Employees are responsible for providing:
- High-speed internet connection (minimum 25 Mbps download, 5 Mbps upload)
- Reliable power supply (surge protector recommended)
- Appropriate workspace furniture (desk, chair)
- Adequate lighting
- Climate control (heating/cooling)
- Phone line if needed for role

5.4 Equipment Stipend
Full-time remote employees receive a one-time home office equipment stipend of $500 to purchase:
- Ergonomic chair
- Desk or workspace furniture
- Additional lighting
- Webcam upgrade
- Other workspace items to enhance productivity

Stipend is provided after successful completion of trial period. Receipts must be submitted for reimbursement.

5.5 Internet and Phone Reimbursement
Full-time remote employees receive:
- Monthly internet reimbursement of $50
- Mobile phone reimbursement of $75 for roles requiring significant phone use
- Reimbursements are taxable income unless documented as used exclusively for business

5.6 Technical Support
- IT Help Desk provides remote technical support during business hours
- Response time for remote worker issues: 4 hours for critical, 24 hours for non-critical
- Employees must troubleshoot basic issues before contacting IT
- Equipment failures may require temporary work from office or another location while repairs are arranged
- Backup equipment may be provided for critical roles
- Remote employees should maintain personal device as backup for emergency communication

5.7 Equipment Security and Care
Employees must:
- Keep all equipment secure and prevent unauthorized access
- Use equipment only for Company business purposes
- Report lost, stolen, or damaged equipment immediately
- Not install unauthorized software or make modifications
- Return equipment immediately upon request or termination
- Allow Company access to equipment for maintenance, updates, or investigations

SECTION 6: SECURITY, CONFIDENTIALITY, AND DATA PROTECTION

6.1 Information Security Requirements
Remote workers must comply with all Company information security policies:
- Use only Company-approved devices for accessing Company systems and data
- Enable and use VPN for all remote connections to Company network
- Keep all security software, patches, and updates current
- Use strong, unique passwords and enable multi-factor authentication
- Lock devices when stepping away and power off when not in use
- Encrypt sensitive data stored on devices
- Disable automatic login features
- Be vigilant against phishing, social engineering, and cyber threats

6.2 Network Security
- Do not use public Wi-Fi networks for Company work without VPN
- Home Wi-Fi must be password-protected with WPA2 or WPA3 encryption
- Change default router passwords and keep router firmware updated
- Use separate guest network for personal devices if possible
- Do not allow others to use Company devices or access Company systems

6.3 Physical Security of Information
- Keep confidential documents and materials secure
- Store sensitive documents in locked drawer or cabinet when not in use
- Shred confidential documents when no longer needed
- Position screens away from windows and sight lines of others
- Be mindful of others who may overhear confidential conversations
- Do not leave devices or documents in vehicles or public places

6.4 Confidentiality and Privacy
- Remote work does not change confidentiality obligations
- Ensure privacy during confidential calls and video meetings
- Use headphones for sensitive conversations
- Do not discuss confidential matters in presence of household members or others
- Be aware of background noise, conversations, and visible materials during video calls
- Close confidential documents when others are present

6.5 Data Backup
- Ensure all work product is saved to Company network drives or cloud storage
- Do not store work files solely on local device
- Sync files regularly to ensure backup
- Contact IT immediately if data loss occurs

6.6 Incident Reporting
Report security incidents immediately to IT and Information Security:
- Lost or stolen devices
- Suspected malware or virus infections
- Phishing attempts or social engineering
- Unauthorized access attempts
- Data breaches or inadvertent disclosure
- Any other security concerns

SECTION 7: HEALTH, SAFETY, AND ERGONOMICS

7.1 Workplace Safety
Employees are responsible for maintaining a safe home workspace:
- Keep workspace free of hazards (tripping, fire, electrical)
- Ensure adequate egress and fire safety
- Maintain smoke detectors and fire extinguishers
- Keep electrical cords and equipment in good condition
- Report any work-related injuries or illnesses immediately
- Comply with workers' compensation requirements

7.2 Ergonomics and Wellness
To prevent injury and promote health:
- Position monitor at arm's length, top of screen at eye level
- Keep keyboard and mouse at elbow height
- Sit with feet flat on floor or footrest
- Maintain neutral wrist and arm positions
- Use ergonomic chair with lumbar support
- Take regular breaks (5-10 minutes every hour)
- Practice eye exercises and stretching
- Maintain good posture throughout the day

Ergonomic assessments and consultations are available from HR upon request.

7.3 Mental Health and Well-Being
Remote work can present unique challenges to mental health:
- Maintain work-life boundaries; end work at designated time
- Create routines and rituals to signal start and end of workday
- Take breaks and step away from workspace
- Stay connected with colleagues through regular communication
- Participate in virtual social activities and team building
- Reach out for support when feeling isolated or stressed
- Use Employee Assistance Program (EAP) for counseling and resources
- Discuss workload concerns with manager proactively

SECTION 8: LEGAL AND COMPLIANCE CONSIDERATIONS

8.1 Employment Laws and Regulations
- Remote employees are subject to all applicable federal, state, and local employment laws
- Company complies with wage and hour laws, including overtime requirements
- Work performed in employee's location is subject to that jurisdiction's laws
- Employees must notify HR of any change in work location

8.2 Tax Implications
- Remote work may create tax obligations in employee's work state
- Company will withhold taxes according to employee's work location
- Employees may be subject to income tax in multiple states
- Employees are responsible for understanding personal tax implications
- Consult tax advisor for guidance on home office deductions and other matters

8.3 Workers' Compensation
- Workers' compensation coverage extends to remote work during work hours
- Injuries occurring during work time and in connection with work duties are covered
- Report all work-related injuries immediately to manager and HR
- Injuries occurring during personal activities are not covered

8.4 Liability and Insurance
- Company liability insurance covers remote work activities
- Employees' homeowner/renter insurance typically does not cover business equipment or liability
- Employees should review their insurance policies and consider additional coverage if desired
- Company is not responsible for damage to employee's personal property

SECTION 9: EXPENSES AND REIMBURSEMENT

9.1 Reimbursable Expenses
The Company will reimburse the following remote work expenses:
- Internet service: $50/month for full-time remote employees
- Mobile phone: $75/month for eligible roles
- Home office setup: One-time $500 stipend
- Office supplies: Up to $25/month with receipts
- Shipping and postage for business needs
- Printing services when required (must be pre-approved)

9.2 Non-Reimbursable Expenses
The Company will not reimburse:
- Mortgage, rent, utilities (except internet as specified)
- Home maintenance or repairs
- Furniture or equipment beyond approved stipend
- Home office renovations or improvements
- Personal supplies or expenses
- Commuting costs for required office visits
- Travel to office location for employees who relocated away from office area

9.3 Expense Submission Process
- Submit expense reports through the expense management system
- Attach receipts and documentation for all expenses
- Describe business purpose clearly
- Submit monthly or as expenses are incurred
- Expenses submitted more than 90 days after incurred may not be reimbursed

SECTION 10: MANAGEMENT AND SUPERVISION OF REMOTE TEAMS

10.1 Manager Responsibilities
Managers of remote employees should:
- Clearly communicate expectations, goals, and deadlines
- Establish regular check-in meetings (1-on-1s and team meetings)
- Focus on results and outputs rather than activity monitoring
- Provide timely feedback, recognition, and coaching
- Ensure equitable treatment of remote and in-office employees
- Foster team cohesion and inclusive culture
- Address performance issues promptly and directly
- Support professional development and career growth
- Be accessible and responsive to remote team members
- Trust employees while maintaining accountability

10.2 Building Remote Team Culture
Strategies for maintaining strong team culture:
- Schedule regular team meetings with cameras on
- Create opportunities for informal interaction (virtual coffee chats)
- Celebrate achievements, milestones, and birthdays
- Include remote employees in all team activities and decisions
- Use collaborative tools for brainstorming and ideation
- Rotate meeting times to accommodate different time zones
- Document decisions and discussions for those who can't attend
- Encourage knowledge sharing and mentorship
- Plan occasional in-person gatherings when possible

10.3 Performance Management
- Set clear, measurable goals and key performance indicators
- Conduct regular performance check-ins (at least monthly)
- Document performance discussions and outcomes
- Address performance issues early and directly
- Provide ongoing coaching and development opportunities
- Conduct formal performance reviews on standard schedule
- Ensure remote employees have equal access to promotions and advancement

10.4 Monitoring and Oversight
The Company respects employee privacy while ensuring accountability:
- Performance is measured by results, not activity monitoring
- Company may monitor use of Company systems, devices, and networks
- Company does not use webcams or tracking software to monitor remote employees
- Attendance and availability during core hours are expected
- Time tracking is required for non-exempt employees

SECTION 11: HYBRID WORK BEST PRACTICES

11.1 Scheduling In-Office Days
- Coordinate with team to maximize collaboration on in-office days
- Use in-office time for activities that benefit from in-person interaction
- Schedule heads-down focused work for remote days
- Block calendar for deep work time
- Be flexible when teammates need to adjust schedules

11.2 Desk and Space Management
- Hot-desking may be used in offices with predominantly hybrid workforce
- Reserve workspace in advance using desk booking system
- Clean and sanitize workspace before and after use
- Do not leave personal items in shared spaces
- Store belongings in assigned lockers or take home daily

11.3 Seamless Transitions
To move smoothly between office and remote work:
- Use cloud storage for all files to access from any location
- Keep equipment and supplies in both locations or transport as needed
- Test technology before important remote meetings
- Maintain consistent work routines regardless of location
- Communicate your location and schedule clearly

SECTION 12: POLICY COMPLIANCE AND ENFORCEMENT

12.1 Employee Acknowledgment
Employees approved for remote work must:
- Sign Remote Work Agreement acknowledging Policy
- Complete Remote Work Training module
- Review and acknowledge updates to Policy annually
- Comply with all provisions of this Policy

12.2 Violations and Consequences
Violations of this Policy may result in:
- Verbal or written warnings
- Modification or termination of remote work arrangement
- Requirement to return to office work
- Disciplinary action up to and including termination
- Legal action for serious violations (theft, security breaches)

Common violations include:
- Unauthorized location or relocation without approval
- Failure to be available during core hours
- Missing meetings or deadlines
- Security policy violations
- Allowing others to use Company equipment
- Working outside approved jurisdiction
- Misrepresenting work hours or location

12.3 Dispute Resolution
Concerns or disputes regarding remote work should be addressed:
1. First, discuss with immediate manager
2. If unresolved, escalate to department director
3. Contact Human Resources for guidance and mediation
4. Follow standard grievance procedures as outlined in Employee Handbook
